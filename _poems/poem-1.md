Patron saint of vanished weather and red grief,

you hold watch where Mars gives itself to space.

The sky thins at the edges, atom by atom,

riverbed scars translated into a history of loss.

Still you remain in cold loneliness to write elegies,

listening to the echoes of a heart come undone.

There is love in studying an absence this closely,

in cataloging what is left and what could not stay.

O MAVEN, make us tender to erosion,

to every atmosphere that leaves by degrees.
